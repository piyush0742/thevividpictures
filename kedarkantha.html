About Kedarkantha Trek

Kedarkantha, standing majestically at 12,500 feet, is one of India's most popular winter treks and a perfect introduction to high-altitude Himalayan adventures. Located in the Govind Wildlife Sanctuary in Uttarkashi district of Uttarakhand, this stunning peak offers an incredible 360-degree view of the Himalayan ranges including Bandarpoonch, Swargarohini, Black Peak, and Ranglana.

The trek is renowned for its pristine snow-covered landscapes during winter months, making it a winter wonderland for adventure enthusiasts. The journey takes you through dense pine forests, frozen lakes, charming Himalayan villages, and vast meadows before reaching the spectacular summit. The trek typically spans 6 days and covers approximately 20 kilometers of diverse terrain.

What makes Kedarkantha truly special is its accessibility for beginners while still offering the thrill of a proper Himalayan summit. The trek provides an perfect blend of challenge and beauty, with each day bringing new landscapes and experiences. From the base village of Sankri to the summit, trekkers are treated to some of the most photogenic views in the Indian Himalayas.
